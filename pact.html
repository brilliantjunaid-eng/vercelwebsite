When starting feels heavy

The first fifteen minutes are usually the hardest.

You are not lazy for struggling there. Most students lose the session before the real work even begins.

What procrastination feels like

You sit down, open the book, and still cannot enter.

The problem is rarely effort itself. It is that strange moment before effort, when everything else feels easier than beginning.

What this app tries to do

Make your mind lean forward before the work starts.

Not with guilt. Not with loud gamification. Just enough curiosity and calm structure to help you cross the threshold.

What happens next

1. Start with one small promise Decide what this block is and what it is not. That lowers the chaos before the work starts.
2. Name the topic and the first reachable step The clearer the opening move is, the less room procrastination has to bargain with you.
3. Let curiosity do some of the lifting Before the chapter feels difficult, let it feel interesting enough to approach.
4. Protect one short sprint You do not need to win the whole day. You only need to stay with the next honest stretch of attention.
